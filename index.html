The Model

  • Infinite Horizontal Plane of Fluid
    • Under the Boussinesq Approximation
  • Heated From Below
  • In a Gravitational Field

The Model: The Math

$$ \begin{aligned} &\frac{1}{P}\left\lbrack\frac{\partial\mathbf{v}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{v}\cdot\nabla)\mathbf{v}\right\rbrack = -\nabla\overline{\omega} + R\theta\mathbf{\hat z} + \nabla^2\mathbf{v}, \label{rb1}\\ &\frac{\partial\theta}{\partial t} = v_z - \mathbf{v}\cdot\nabla\theta + \nabla^2\theta, \label{rb2}\\ &\nabla\cdot\mathbf{v} = 0, \label{rb3}\\ &\text{where}\\ &R \propto \Delta T \in \mathbb{R}, \hspace{1em} P \in \mathbb{R}, \hspace{1em} \overline{\omega} \sim \frac{p'}{\rho_0} \end{aligned} $$

Linearization & Analysis

  • Linearize about \(\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{0}, ~\theta = 0\)
  • Apply \(\mathbf{\hat z}(\nabla\times\nabla\times)\) to the first eq.
  • Eliminate \(x, y, t\) dependence with Laplacian and Fourier Transforms
  • Eliminate \(\theta\) and achieve an ODE on \(v_z\)
  • Find the eigenvalues \(\lambda_n\)
  • Find conditions on \(\mathcal{R}[\lambda_n] < 0\)

Stability Condition

$$ R < R_n(k) \equiv \frac{(n^2\pi^2 + k^2)^3}{k^2} $$
